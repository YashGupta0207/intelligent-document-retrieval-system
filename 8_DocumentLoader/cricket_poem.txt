Cricket is not just a game we play,
It’s a rhythm of hope in bright daylight.
From dusty streets to emerald grounds,
Dreams rise with every bat’s sound.

The pitch lies calm before the storm,
Grass whispering tales yet to form.
White lines mark more than space,
They draw the limits of faith and grace.

A bat rests light in eager hands,
Carrying prayers of distant lands.
The ball gleams red or shining white,
A comet ready to challenge might.

The bowler runs with measured breath,
Each step defying doubt and death.
Arm swings true, the seam bites air,
A question flung with silent dare.

The batsman waits, eyes sharp and still,
Heart pounding louder than his will.
In that brief pause before release,
Time itself seems forced to cease.

Crack of willow, a perfect sound,
Echoing joy across the ground.
The ball escapes, a racing thought,
Freedom earned, not merely caught.

A cover drive, poetry in motion,
Flowing smooth as rolling ocean.
A pull shot fierce, raw and wild,
The crowd erupts, a roaring child.

Fielders dive with fearless grace,
Chasing glory in open space.
Every save, every flying stop,
Can tilt the game, can make hearts drop.

The keeper crouches, hawk-like keen,
Guarding stumps with watchful sheen.
A nick, a flash, gloves snap tight,
A wicket falls in sheer delight.

Runs are stitched with sweat and pain,
Singles stolen in pouring rain.
Between the creases, trust is born,
Two souls sprinting, breathless, worn.

The scoreboard ticks like fate’s own clock,
Each number adding gentle shock.
A century blooms from patient art,
A milestone carved by mind and heart.

The crowd becomes a living sea,
Waves of hope and agony.
Flags rise high, voices blend,
Belief that this moment will never end.

Captains plot with furrowed brow,
Changing fields, adjusting how.
Mind games play where muscles tire,
Strategy feeding inner fire.

Tea breaks pass, the sun dips low,
Shadows stretch, the lights now glow.
Still the battle holds its line,
A test of spirit, slow, divine.

In final overs, nerves run thin,
Every breath a fight to win.
One last ball, the world stands still,
History bends to human will.

Win or lose, the truth remains,
Cricket binds our joys and pains.
More than sport, it shapes our soul,
A timeless game that makes us whole.
